Gulab Jamun

Mix milk powder, flour, and baking soda.

Add milk to form a dough.

Shape into small balls.

Heat ghee in a pan and fry the balls until golden.

Soak in warm sugar syrup flavored with cardamom.

Interesting Fact:

Gulab Jamun is believed to have Persian origins and was brought to India by the Mughals. The name "Gulab" comes from the Persian word for rosewater, which is often used in the syrup. The sweet's popularity soared in the Indian subcontinent, becoming a staple at festive occasions and celebrations.

Rasgulla

Boil milk and curdle with lemon juice.

Strain and rinse the curdled milk.

Knead the chenna until smooth.

Shape into balls and boil in sugar syrup.

Let cool and absorb syrup.

Interesting Fact:

Rasgulla, also known as Rasgola, originated in the state of West Bengal, India. The sweet is made from chenna or curdled milk and is famous for its spongy texture. Legend has it that Rasgulla was first created by a Bengali sweet maker in the 19th century, and it has since become a beloved dessert across India.

Jalebi

Prepare a batter with flour, yogurt, and baking powder.

Heat oil for frying.

Pipe batter into hot oil in a spiral shape.

Fry until crispy.

Soak in warm sugar syrup.

Interesting Fact:

Jalebi is a popular sweet in India and other parts of South Asia. Its origins can be traced back to ancient Persia, where it was known as 'Zulbia'. The vibrant color and crisp texture of Jalebi make it a festive treat, often enjoyed during religious celebrations and special occasions.

Kheer

Boil rice with milk.

Add sugar and cook until thickened.

Stir in cardamom and saffron.

Garnish with nuts.

Serve warm or chilled.

Interesting Fact:

Kheer is a traditional Indian rice pudding that is enjoyed across the subcontinent. It has variations in different regions, with some adding ingredients like raisins, coconut, or dry fruits. The dish is often prepared during festivals like Diwali and is considered a symbol of prosperity and joy.

Barfi

Cook condensed milk and milk powder together.

Stir continuously to avoid lumps.

Add ghee and cook until the mixture thickens.

Pour into a greased tray.

Cut into pieces when set.

Interesting Fact:

Barfi, also known as Burfi, is a rich, milk-based sweet that originated in India. It is commonly flavored with cardamom, nuts, and saffron. Barfi is often served at weddings and special occasions, and its variety of flavors and textures makes it a popular choice for celebrations.
